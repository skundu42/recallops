# Engineering Onboarding

## First week

New engineers receive laptop, accounts, and access requests before day one. The
first week covers environment setup, the architecture walkthrough, and a starter
task sized to ship within five days. Every new hire is paired with an onboarding
buddy for the first month.

## Access and permissions

Production access is granted only after completing the security training and the
on-call shadowing checklist. Access requests use the least-privilege template;
broad grants require a written justification and expire after 90 days by default.

## The starter task

Starter tasks are chosen from the good-first-issue backlog: small, user-visible,
and touching the deploy pipeline end to end. The goal is a merged pull request and
a production deploy in week one, not maximal difficulty.

## 30-60-90 expectations

At 30 days: shipping independently within the team's codebase. At 60 days: leading
a small project with a design doc. At 90 days: participating in the on-call
rotation with a completed shadowing record and a passed incident drill.

## Feedback loops

Managers hold weekly one-on-ones from week one. A structured check-in at 45 days
covers progress against the 30-60-90 plan, with course corrections written down
and revisited at 90 days.
